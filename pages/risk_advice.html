What it shows: Risk of pension funds struggling to rise - or falling - from current levels.

Why it matters: If you know when risks of falls are low and when they are high, it becomes possible to use risk-balancing strategies to reduce occasional losses.

How to use it: This tool provides a simple guide to risk levels faced by equity-based pension funds. It can be used in two ways. Simplest is to divert new pension savings contributions into cash when risk is high. This simple strategy is to save more of your monthly contribution to safer funds (gilts, bonds or cash-equivalents) when risks are high, and commit more of your contributions to equity funds when risks are lower. More sophisticated savers may also want to sell equity funds when risks are very high.

What next?

This tool's recommendations give an idea of how to use it for risk management. Use it to learn about managing risk - or as a basis for conversations with your Independent Financial Adviser about reducing risk.
